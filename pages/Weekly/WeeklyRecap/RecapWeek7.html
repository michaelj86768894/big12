Did you draft Rico Dowdle this year? Of course you didn’t. (Don’t lie to me! We got the receipts.) But that’s okay! Neither did most fantasy football players, since Dowdle was expected to be Chuba Hubbard’s backup for the lowly Panthers. And neither did any NFL teams, as Dowdle was an undrafted free agent back in 2020. He spent four years climbing up the Cowboys’ depth chart...only for them to let him walk in free agency this offseason, as part of their franchise strategy of saving up money to not pay Micah Parsons.

In Weeks 5 and 6, Dowdle has come out of nowhere and emerged as a fantasy god. Last week Dowdle had a career-high 207 yards against the Dolphins, and issued a warning ahead of this week’s matchup against the Cowboys: “Buckle up.”

But, uh, Jerry Jones traded away the seatbelts. Dowdle went for 239 scrimmage yards and two touchdowns, leading the entire NFL in fantasy scoring. Not just running backs — all players! Rico Freakin’ Dowdle? The front-runner for Fantasy MVP!

It’s a story that reminds me of the Boston Brawlers, who had the biggest week-over-week improvement in the league. After last week’s performance, they were disrespected, unwanted, and unloved. Especially if Colvin and the Bolts defeated them this week. But look at them now, getting a big writeup in the newsletter.

In some ways, it’s totally unnecessary to provide further context on the New York Jets. They are 0-6. After several hours of hacking into ESPN's fanciest algorithms and advanced analytics databases, I have determined that is not only the fewest wins of any NFL team this season, but of any NFL team ever.

But it actually does get worse. Justin Fields completed nine passes for 45 yards yesterday, but lost 55 yards on nine sacks. That left the Jets with -10 passing yards on Sunday, the fewest in an NFL game since 1998. To be clear: That’s not “ten passing yards,” that’s “negative ten passing yards.” That little sign in front is a minus, a fact I learned in 9th grade math at Tech.

Here’s an incomplete list of people who had more passing yards than the New York Jets on Sunday:

  • me, the person writing this newletter
  • you, the person reading this newsletter
  • Vegas GM, Dave Nguyen, even though their fantasy team absolutely sucks this year
  • Celine Dion
  • Most babies
  • Longtime MLB utility infielder Mark Grudzielanek
  • Petey Pablo
  • Lisa, Felicia, Monique, and all the other women mentioned in Petey Pablo’s 2003 classic “Freek-a-Leek”
  • Brandon Weeden

The Jets’ performance reminded me of the Miami Inferno and their 152.40-point performance. Are they good at drafting? No. Are they good at finding useful players over the course of the season? Also no. Are they putting the players on their roster in the best position to succeed? Gonna be honest: No. Do they have any hope this season? I’m leaning no. Should the person in charge of this team be publicly shamed until they relinquish control of this moribund franchise? I’ll let you make the call. Between them and Vegas, I don't know who's worse.

Another league favorite right now is Giants running back Cam Skattebo. Most NFL running backs correctly believe their job is to run past defenders; Skattebo is one of the beautiful, demented few who voluntarily runs into them, like he’s trying to achieve the high score in a CTE-themed video game. On his second of three touchdowns against the Eagles, Skattebo actively passed up the opportunity to run into the end zone untouched and made sure he hit a Philadelphia defender.

Skattebo’s teammate, Gunner Olszewski, described Skattebo as “genuinely an idiot.”
