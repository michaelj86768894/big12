Week 3 Preview

Can Mike McDaniel take his own advice tonight?

So far this season I have been preaching caution. It’s still early and it’s critical to not overreact to a small sample size, especially as it relates to rookies. They’re still learning where the bathrooms are, for Pete’s sake! But that preaching has come to an end. For me to dole out advice that resembles “don’t panic” would make me a hypocrite. I am panicking.

However, I have found comfort in the words of two head football coaches. Over the weekend, Clemson head coach Dabo Swinney gave me some guidance about how to deal mentally with an underperforming group. When one of your leagumates text you about how you are 0-2 or you got blown out by 90 points just copy and paste this back.

Yeah, we a little down right now. Take your shots. But I got a long memory, in case y'all don't know. We'll be aight. We'll bounce back. This is a progrum built to last. Always has been. Always will be. And I would just say if you don't believe in us because we lost two games, then you didn't believe in us anyway, so it don't matter! You wasn't all in anyway! If you're all in, you burn the ships, man! There ain't no exit strategy. Like, you're freakin' all in - Dabo Swinney

And then there's Mike McDaniel. His Dolphins play the Bills tonight in what virtually nobody is expecting to be a good game. But Mike has an ace in the hole. He has found the secret. If you're reading this, I recommend instilling this advice in your fantasy squad this week.

To win games you have to win the game, not lose the game. - Mike McDaniel

I see a lot of people making fun of Mike for this quote, but he is correct. I have always found this to be the case.

Alright, let’s dive into this week’s matchups!

Philadelphia is looking to break a five-game losing streak against Boston, but with Boston standing as the league’s lone undefeated team and one of the top-scoring squads this season, it won’t be easy. Cedar Rapids also faces a tough challenge, trying to snap a five-game skid versus Cleveland after Cleveland’s record-setting performance last week.

Milwaukee aims to keep their momentum going against Chicago, riding a three-game winning streak — though Chicago has struggled this season, with a 3-19 power ranking record that puts them near the bottom of the league. Miami (1-1) vs. Brooklyn earns the “Might Be Tough to Watch” award this week, as both teams sit in the bottom half of the league in scoring and Brooklyn is still winless.

The marquee matchup of the week features Los Angeles (1-1) against Northern Arizona (1-1). Both teams are in the top half for scoring, and they’re just two of four squads to have scored 200+ points in a single game this season. LA is also chasing their 100th regular-season win in franchise history. Finally, Tucson is looking to notch their 100th win (including playoffs) as they square off against Vegas.

Week 3 is loaded with streaks on the line, milestone wins within reach, and a couple of games that could get ugly fast. From Philly and Cedar Rapids trying to shake off history, to LA and Northern Arizona battling for early-season bragging rights, every matchup has something riding on it. Cast your votes, lock in your picks, and get ready — this week could start shaping the contenders from the pretenders.
