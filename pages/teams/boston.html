The Boston Brawlers are one of the original franchises in the Big 12, debuting in 2012 under GM Antoine Lee-Thomas. The early years were marked by significant struggles, with the team finishing 4–10 in its inaugural season and posting a league-worst 0–14 record in 2013. A change in leadership came in 2014, when John Udvare took over as GM, sparking a gradual turnaround.

Under Udvare’s guidance, Boston became a competitive force, reaching the playoffs in three consecutive seasons (2015–2017). Despite strong regular seasons—highlighted by back-to-back 8–5 campaigns in 2016 and 2017—the Brawlers failed to notch a postseason victory, exiting in the first round each time.

In 2018, the franchise suspended operations after Commissioner Michael Bautch was unable to find a replacement GM in time for the season. The team returned in 2019, starting with Travis Gebel at GM before a midseason controversy led to Chris Cholka taking the helm. Under Cholka, Boston stabilized, clinching a playoff berth in 2019 before posting consistent 6–8 seasons from 2021 to 2023.

The Brawlers’ best modern-era performance came in 2024, when they finished 9–5, captured a division title, and set a franchise points record in the playoffs despite falling to the eventual champion Tucson Sugar Skulls in a high-scoring first-round matchup (225–260). As the league transitions to dynasty play in 2026, Boston enters with a renewed sense of identity and a reputation as one of the league’s most resilient franchises.
