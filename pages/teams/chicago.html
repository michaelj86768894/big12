The Chicago Slaughter have been a staple of the Big 12 since its inception, known for their resilience and a gradual rise from early struggles to a long-awaited championship appearance in 2024. Despite a slow start in the franchise’s early years, GM Darrien Clay has remained at the helm for the entire run, becoming one of the league’s most enduring leaders.

The team’s formative years were challenging, with losing records from 2012 to 2014, never finishing above 6-8 during that span. In 2015, Chicago broke through with an 8-5 record and their first playoff berth, though they fell in the opening round to Northern Arizona. They returned to the postseason in 2016, only to suffer another first-round exit, this time to Milwaukee.

After a middling 2017, the Slaughter benefited from league realignment in 2018 (moving to the East Division), posting an 8-6 record, winning the division and advancing to the second round before bowing out to Vegas. The following year marked a peak in the pre-pandemic era: Chicago captured the Central Division title in 2019 with a 9-4 record, though Brooklyn ended their run in the semifinals.

The 2020 season cancellation was followed by a sharp decline, as Chicago stumbled to 2-12 in 2021, their worst record in franchise history. Gradually, the team regained form, earning playoff berths in 2023 and 2024, the latter being their most successful season to date. That year, Chicago finished 8-6, stormed through the postseason with decisive wins over Vegas and Los Angeles, and reached the championship game before falling to Tucson in a high-scoring battle, 195-233.

Despite years of ups and downs, Chicago has become synonymous with perseverance and steady leadership. With five playoff appearances in the last seven active seasons and a near-miss title in 2024, the Slaughter are widely viewed as a contender on the brink of securing their first championship.
