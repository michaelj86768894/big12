The 2025 season marks the 13th year of the Big 12 Fantasy League, and it kicked off in style on Thursday, September 4th. Last year’s champions, the Tucson Sugar Skulls, clashed with their division rivals, the Los Angeles Wildcats, in one of the most thrilling opening night games the league has seen. Both teams shattered their projected scores as the win probability swung back and forth until the final horn, with Los Angeles pulling out a 213.1 to 192.25 victory.

The season will run through Sunday, December 28th, culminating in the Big 12 Championship in Santa Clara, California.

No front office drama to report this year—every GM is back at the helm, ready to run it back for another wild ride. The only shake-up came from the reigning champs, the Green Bay Blizzard, who stunned the league with a full rebrand to the Tucson Sugar Skulls.

Some say it’s a bold move to shed a championship identity, others think it’s just an intimidation tactic—because nothing says “we’re coming for you again” like trading snowflakes for skulls. Either way, the Sugar Skulls enter 2025 with a target on their backs and plenty of swagger.

League insiders were quick to react:

  • “Changing the name after winning it all? That’s like Jordan dropping the Bulls logo for a skull tattoo. Respect.” – Anonymous GM
  • “They may be called the Sugar Skulls, but there’s nothing sweet about playing them.” – X User @Blizzardfan4lyfe
  • “Classic move—win a title, then try to sell more merch.” – League cynic


The 2025 Big 12 Draft kicked off on Monday, September 1st at 7:30 PM—marking the final draft of the re-draft era before the league transitions to dynasty in 2026. Draft nominations were determined randomly one hour before kickoff, giving Los Angeles the #1 pick and Vegas the final nomination of the evening.

The auction draft delivered everything fans love: bold moves, surprise reaches, and a handful of outright steals. Highlights included:

  • First player signed: Ja’Mar Chase, $79, by Vegas
  • Highest paid player: Jammer Gibbs, $83, by Tucson
  • Last player signed: Paulson Adebo, by Vegas

It was a fitting finale to over a decade of re-draft tradition, and the stage is officially set for the 2025 season kickoff.

The 2025 season spanned 14 weeks and 168 games, kicking off on the Thursday night following Labor Day—a staple tradition for the league’s opening night. Each team squared off twice against division rivals and once against all other teams, with the final week subject to flex scheduling to keep playoff races and divisional championships competitive.

For those still nostalgic about the experimental days, the league maintained three individual defensive positions (IDP) in lieu of team defenses—a nod to the innovation introduced back in 2022, though many GMs might barely remember the switch at this point.

Continuing a long-standing trend, the league embraced flex scheduling for the final week of the regular season, a strategy that added drama to playoff seeding and divisional matchups. “Nothing spices up Week 14 like moving games around and watching the standings shuffle,” quipped one anonymous GM. Another added, “Flex scheduling is basically fantasy football’s version of musical chairs—and I live for it.”

The 2025 postseason kicks off on December 14, 2025, culminating in the Big 12 Championship on December 30 in Santa Clara, California. Seven teams earned the right to compete: divisional winners claimed automatic spots, while the remaining four slots went to the best non-division winners.

The playoff structure gives the #1 seed a first-round bye, while matchups for the opening weekend are as follows:

  • #2 seed vs. #7 seed
  • #3 seed vs. #6 seed
  • #4 seed vs. #5 seed

Home teams enjoy a small advantage with a 5-point bonus to start each week, adding a layer of strategy and excitement to the matchups.
