The 2014 season marked the league's third year of competition in the Big 12. The season commenced on Thursday, September 4, 2014, with the defending champions, Brooklyn Bolts, hosting the Miami Inferno. In a high-scoring encounter, the Inferno secured a notable 150-113 victory. The season reached its culmination on Monday, December 22, 2014, with the Milwaukee Mustangs emerging victorious in the Big 12 Championship by defeating the Cleveland Gladiators with a commanding score of 141-118. This triumph marked the Mustangs' inaugural Big 12 championship in the franchise's history.

Following the mid-season departure of GM Antoine Lee-Thomas in 2013, the Boston Brawlers underwent a change in leadership for the 2014 season. The team appointed John Udvare as the new General Manager, entrusting him with guiding the Brawlers through the challenges and opportunities of the upcoming season.

The 2014 draft marked the league's 3rd annual gathering of Big 12 General Managers to draft eligible football players. The event unfolded in Wauwatosa, WI, at the former headquarters of the Milwaukee Mustangs on September 5, 2014, commencing at 4:00 pm CST. The honor of the first overall pick belonged to the Vegas Knight Hawks, who opted to select Adrian Peterson from Minnesota as their top choice. Notably, this marked the second consecutive year that Peterson was chosen with the coveted 1st overall pick.

The 2014 regular season, shortened from 14 to 13 weeks, featured 156 games played across the condensed schedule that commenced on the Thursday night following Labor Day. Each Big 12 team participated in one game per week. Despite their status as defending champions, the Brooklyn Bolts encountered a challenging season, beginning with a disappointing 1-5 record. Ultimately, they concluded the season at 5-8, falling short of the playoffs by two games. On the opposite end of the spectrum, Green Bay emerged as the top team, finishing the regular season with an impressive 11-2 record. Meanwhile, the Vegas Knight Hawks faced a tumultuous season marked by suspensions and injuries, ultimately finishing last with a record of 2-11.

The playoffs for the 2014 season kicked off on December 4, 2014, concluding with the thrilling Big 12 Championship on December 22, where the Milwaukee Mustangs triumphed over the Cleveland Gladiators with a score of 141-118.

Round 1

In the opening round of the 2014 playoffs, held from December 4-8, the 3rd seed Los Angeles Wildcats dominated, securing a comfortable 142-78 victory against the 6th seed Philadelphia Soul.

The weekend's other matchup featured a tight battle between the #5 seed Mustangs and the #4 seed Miami Inferno. The Mustangs managed to clinch the win with a score of 130-122, successfully fending off a late comeback attempt by the Inferno. The Monday night thriller showcased a remarkable 42-point performance by Julio Jones, adding intensity and excitement to the playoff clash.

Round 2

The second round of the 2014 playoffs kicked off on December 11. In a stunning upset, the top-seeded Green Bay Blizzard faced a formidable challenge at home, succumbing to the relentless performance of the Milwaukee Mustangs, who secured a convincing 154-116 victory. The triumph propelled the Mustangs into the Big 12 Championship game for the second consecutive year.

In the weekend's second game, the 2nd seed Cleveland Gladiators defended their home turf efficiently, overcoming the Los Angeles Wildcats with a dominant 134-90 victory. This win secured the Gladiators' inaugural ticket to the championship showdown.

Big 12 Championship

The climax of the 2014 Big 12 season unfolded on December 22, 2014 in Glendale, Arizona, with the highly anticipated championship clash between division rivals the Milwaukee Mustangs and the Cleveland Gladiators. Entering the matchup as a 1-point underdog, the Mustangs faced the formidable Gladiators, against whom they had suffered two previous defeats during the season. This championship bout marked Milwaukee' second consecutive appearance in the title game, following their loss to the Brooklyn Bolts the year before, while Cleveland made their inaugural appearance in the grand finale.

The game took an unexpected turn as AJ Green, a key player for the Gladiators, suffered a leg injury on the first play of the Monday Night Football game, ultimately contributing zero points. This setback proved decisive, sealing the fate of the contest. Despite the odds and the challenges faced, the Milwaukee Mustangs emerged triumphant with a final score of 141-118, securing their first-ever Big 12 championship in team history.

In 2014, Cleveland and Miami celebrated their maiden division titles, marking a significant achievement for both franchises. Meanwhile, Tucson continued their dominance, securing their second consecutive division championship and showcasing sustained excellence.

Drafted in the secondround, Antonio Brown earned the esteemed title of League MVP for the 2014 season, delivering an outstanding performance by accumulating an impressive 380 points. Brown played a pivotal role in guiding the Milwaukee Mustangs to their first-ever league championship.

Paris Reed's remarkable leadership with the Green Bay Blizzard did not go unnoticed, as he was honored with the GM of the Year award. Reed orchestrated the Blizzard's stellar season, culminating in an 11-2 record—the best in the league—and claiming the top spot in the playoffs. This recognition marked Reed's inaugural GM of the Year award, highlighting his exceptional contributions.

On the conference front, Cleveland celebrated their inaugural conference championship, signifying a significant milestone for the franchise. Simultaneously, Milwaukee clinched their second conference championship and secured their first-ever league championship, underscoring their ascent to the pinnacle of Big 12 success.
