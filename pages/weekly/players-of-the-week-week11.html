Wildcats QB Josh Allen, Wranglers RB Christian McCaffrey highlight Players of the Week

Week 11 Players of the Week
Michael Wilson
WR - Milwaukee Mustangs

With Marvin Harrison Jr. (appendectomy) down for the Week 11 matchup versus the 49ers, Wilson was expected to play a larger role in the pass game. But we didn't see Wilson posting a career-high 42.5 points. In a game where the Cards had to chase points, Wilson caught 15 of 18 targets for 185 yards. Big time numbers. And with no timeline set for the return of Harrison, Wilson is set to be the No.1 boundary target again for quarterback Jacoby Brissett in Week 12 against the Jags. Rostered in just 15.2% of ESPN leagues, Wilson should be a priority waiver pick-up.

Josh Allen
QB - Los Angeles Wildcats

He was missing one of his top receivers (Keon Coleman, coach's decision), threw an ugly first-quarter interception in a desperate attempt to avoid a safety and seemed out of rhythm with those who did play in the game's early stages, but Allen delivered the goods for LA. He passed for three touchdowns and ran for three others, and he scored 66.85 fantasy points. He continues to prove himself to be the most dynamic quarterback talent in the league, with a pair of dream matchups coming up on his schedule (@PIT, CIN in Weeks 13-14). For Week 12, Allen's matchup is a tougher one (@HOU), but he's capable of rising above it and delivering a top-five positional score.

Tetairoa McMillan
WR - Brooklyn Bolts

McMillian dropped a career-best 40.0 points on the Falcons defense in Week 11, catching 8 of 12 targets for 130 yards with two touchdown grabs. McMillian, who hadn't found the end zone since Week 6, caught both of his touchdowns versus the Falcons on red zone throws from quarterback Bryce Young. With his frame, and developing route-running traits, the arrow is pointing up on McMillian, who gets the 49ers' defense next in Week 12. He'll be a WR2 for me in the ranks.

Christian McCaffrey
RB - Northern Arizona Wranglers

He and Jonathan Taylor are now ping-ponging the seasonal fantasy points lead, as McCaffrey's 45.1 points gave him 366.15 for the season, 22 more than second-place Taylor. McCaffrey has done it with an additional game -- the big drawback with him in fantasy is that he has a dreaded Week 14 bye -- but he has scored 30-plus in three of his past five games in what has been a stunning, bounce-back year. Expect more top-shelf numbers from him in Week 12 against his former team, the Carolina Panthers.
