Week 10 Recap

Look, I don't want to start your week off with a whole bunch of negativity but I have to be honest, there was some disgusting football played this week. I didn't think it could get much worse than the 10-7 Broncos vs Raiders TNF game but the Eagles and Packers were able to provide a bit of unwanted symmetry by giving us a 10-7 matchup of their own. Gross.

It wasn't totally different around the Big 12. We all had to sit back and watch Milwaukee play Northern Arizona and pretend that we were enjoying ourselves. A deeply unpleasant experience for all involved. But you know what? A win is technically a win. So since nobody else is going to do it, I'd like to congratulate GM Michael Bautch and the Mustangs on being one step closer to the playoffs. Another positive to take out of this week: nobody actually paid attention to this matchup so you didn't put anything on tape. That's critical in fantasy football.

You know what...I'm not done talking about Boston just yet -- today's newsletter is dedicated to them. Sometimes you need to admire, appreciate, and recognize effort. Not talent...I'm not sure they have a whole lot of that. But you know what they do have? Gumption. Grit. A third thing that starts with a G. They worked their ass off this week. Sure, they didn't pick up the win but just know that they are gonna give you everything they've got week in and week out. Like Texans punter Tommy Townsend on Sunday...sure, he may give up a touchdown. But look at the effort displayed in defeat. He starts HAULING ASS at the 45 yard line and you've never seen arm pumping like this in your life. I don't want to get too excited about effort but it's one of the things that excites me the most.

Let's take a minute to talk about the Mustangs. Now I don't want to be one to pooh-pooh success and prosperity. But at the same time, there's a way to go about these things...what they did to the Wranglers just isn't how friends (are they friends?) should treat one another. You've scared them. Now the Wranglers are gonna go out there like this next week. And none of us want to see that...commish might have to step in for player safety.
