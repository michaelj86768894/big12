Franchise History

The Brooklyn Bolts, a charter member of the GLFL fantasy league, have been a perennial contender under the leadership of long-time GM Cory Colvin. Known for their explosive offenses and aggressive in-season moves, the Bolts have carved out a legacy that includes multiple division titles and two Lakefront Bowls (2013, 2021).

Brooklyn debuted in 2012 with an impressive 11-3 record, capturing the East Division crown and advancing to the semifinals before falling to Los Angeles. The following season, the Bolts dominated the league, repeating as division champions and finishing the job with their first Lakefront Bowl in 2013, defeating Milwaukee in a one-sided title game.

After a brief decline in 2014 (5-8) and mixed results through 2016, Brooklyn rebounded with another playoff appearance in 2017, though an early-round exit kept the title out of reach. The Bolts suspended operations during the 2018 season, with Colvin citing “hairline issues” for the hiatus, but returned in 2019 for a dramatic postseason run, reaching the Lakefront Bowl Lakefront Bowl before falling to Miami.

The franchise reached its modern peak in 2021, going 10-4, securing another East Division crown, and winning its second Lakefront Bowl with a decisive victory over Los Angeles. Since then, Brooklyn has battled inconsistency, posting losing or near-.500 records from 2022–2024, including a heartbreaking first-round exit in 2023 to Philadelphia.

Despite recent struggles, the Bolts remain one of the league’s most decorated franchises, boasting five division titles and two Lakefront Bowls over more than a decade of play.
