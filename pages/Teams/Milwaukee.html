Franchise History

The Milwaukee Mustangs are one of the league’s most successful franchises, competing in the Central Division every season under long-time GM Michael Bautch. Known for sustained excellence, Milwaukee has earned six division championships, appeared in four Lakefront Bowl Championship games, and captured two Lakefront Bowl Championships (2014, 2023).

The Mustangs burst onto the scene in 2012 with an 11–3 record and a Central Division crown. They advanced to the conference finals but fell to Tucson. The team continued its dominance in 2013 (10–4) and reached its first championship game, losing to Brooklyn.

In 2014, Milwaukee captured its first league title, defeating Cleveland in the championship behind MVP Antonio Brown. They returned to the title game in 2015 but lost to Las Vegas despite another dominant season. Brown repeated as MVP, and Bautch collected his second GM of the Year award in four seasons.

The Mustangs claimed their second league title in 2023, defeating Northern Arizona in the championship after a strong 8–6 campaign. In 2024, Milwaukee posted a 10–4 record, won another division title, and advanced to the semifinals before falling to Tucson. Bautch earned his third GM of the Year honor that season, cementing the Mustangs as a model of consistency and success.
